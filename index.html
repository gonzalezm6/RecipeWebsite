Bananas Foster French Toast

PREP TIME: 15 Minutes

COOK TIME: 15 Minutes

YIELD: 4 Slices

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs
  • 1/4 tsp. ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 6 Tbs. firmly packed dark brown sugar
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • 4 day-old slices brioche or other egg bread, each 1 inch thick
  • 6 Tbs. (3/4 stick) unsalted butter
  • 4 bananas, cut on the bias into 1/2-inch slices
  • 1/4 cup dark rum
  • 1/2 tsp. vanilla bean paste
  • 3 Tbs. water

Directions

  1. Preheat an oven to 200ºF.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, cinnamon, milk, 1 Tbs. of the brown sugar and a pinch of salt until well combined.
  3. Pour half of the egg mixture into a deep pie dish.
  4. Soak 1 bread slice, turning once, for 1 to 2 minutes. Transfer to a plate. Repeat with the remaining bread and egg mixture.
  5. Gently pour any remaining egg mixture over the bread until absorbed.
  6. In an 11-inch French skillet over medium heat, melt 1 1/2 Tbs. of the butter.
  7. Add 2 bread slices and cook, turning once, until golden, 2 to 3 minutes per side. Transfer to a wire cooling rack set over a baking sheet and keep warm in the oven. Repeat with 1 1/2 Tbs. of the butter and the remaining bread.
  8. In a 9-inch French skillet over medium-high heat, melt the remaining 3 Tbs. butter.
  9. Add the bananas and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned, 2 to 3 minutes.
  10. Carefully pour in the rum, then add the remaining 5 Tbs. brown sugar, the vanilla bean paste, water and a pinch of salt.
  11. Stir to coat the bananas evenly. Simmer until the bananas are soft and the sauce is thickened, 2 to 3 minutes.
  12. Transfer the French toast to individual plates and top with the bananas. Serve immediately. Enjoy!

Eggs in a hole

PREP TIME: 10 Minutes

COOK TIME: 15 Minutes

YIELD: 1 Slice

Ingredients

  • 1/2 tsp. cinnamon
  • 1/8 cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp. butter
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 slice of toast (the thicker the better)
  • 1/8 cup milk
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • Vegetable oil
  • Maple syrup to serve

Directions

  1. Preheat an oven to 325ºF.
  2. Mix the sugar and cinnamon together on a plate, set aside.
  3. Melt the butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Take one of the eggs and whisk it in a medium bowl with the milk and salt.
  5. Quickly dip the bread into the egg mixture and let the excess drip off. You don’t it to seep through the toast.
  6. Use a shot glass (or something of similar size) to cut a hole in the middle of each piece of bread. Save the middle.
  7. Place on the skillet and cook on each side for a few minutes until golden brown.
  8. Preheat the oven to 325ºF.
  9. Remove the bread from the skillet and toss them on the plate with the sugar to coat.
  10. Place on a baking pan lightly coated with vegetable oil.
  11. Crack the 2 remaining eggs on a flat surface and drop in the middle of each hole (the flat surface prevents the shell from being pushed inside).
  12. Bake for approximately 15 minutes, until the egg whites have firmed. Serve with maple syrup and enjoy!

French Toast Dippers

PREP TIME: 15 Minutes

COOK TIME: 15 Minutes

YIELD: 8 Rolls

Ingredients

  • 8 slices soft white bread
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp. cinnamon
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup milk
  • 1/4 tsp. nutmeg
  • 2 tbsp. butter
  • Maple syrup, for dunking

Directions

  1. Slice off crusts of bread and roll flat with a rolling pin.
  2. In a small bowl, stir together melted butter, sugar, and cinnamon.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, and nutmeg.
  4. Slather cinnamon mixture on bread and roll up tightly. Dunk roll-ups in egg mixture.
  5. In a nonstick skillet over medium heat, melt 2 tablespoons butter. Add roll-ups and cook until golden, about 2 minutes per side.
  6. Serve with maple syrup for dipping.
